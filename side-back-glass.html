Bonded Glass — Sealed the Right Way

Most modern side and rear glass is bonded directly to the body with urethane adhesive rather than sitting in a rubber surround. If it isn't cut out and re-bonded correctly, you get wind noise, water leaks and rust down the track. We remove the damaged panel cleanly, prepare the aperture, and bond the new glass to manufacturer specification so it stays weather-tight.

For older vehicles and many cargo models, glass is set into a rubber seal — we replace those too, with fresh seals where needed. Smashed glass in the cabin or load space? We vacuum it out so there's no loose glass left behind.

🔒 Security & weatherproofing: A broken window leaves your vehicle exposed and lets water in. We carry common glass and source the rest quickly to get you secure again fast.

Heated Glass & Wipers

Where your rear glass has a heating element (demister) or a wiper, we replace it with the correct heated/wiper-ready unit and reconnect everything so it works just as it did before.
